vs. Cloudiness

Continents tend to be less cloudy than adjacent oceans (at the same latitude), except in the equatorial belt. On average, cloudiness is highest around 60 deg S (about 80% of the time) but there is only 40% cloud cover at the South Pole. These figures reflect the ascent and subsidence occurring at these respective latitudes, and the large amount of ocean at around 60 deg S. Satellite-based cloudiness estimates over surfaces covered by snow or ice are somewhat uncertain, especially at night.
