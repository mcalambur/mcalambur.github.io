The trade winds (also called trades) are the prevailing pattern of easterly surface winds found in the tropics near the Earth's equator. Surface winds are generally westerly poleward of 30 deg, except near the poles. Midlatitude westerly winds become stronger aloft in the troposphere, on account of thermal wind balance
